Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about BioActiveGlo products, gardening applications, legal compliance, and partnership opportunities.

Important: All BioActiveGlo products are for gardening use only — not for human consumption. No health or medical claims are made anywhere on this site.

Gardening Questions

What is fulvic acid and how does it benefit my garden?

Fulvic acid is a small molecular weight organic compound derived from the natural decomposition of organic matter. In gardening applications, it functions as a chelating agent that binds to mineral nutrients and improves their uptake into plant root systems. It also contributes to soil microbiome health by providing carbon energy for beneficial microorganisms. BioActiveGlo products are formulated for gardening use only and are not intended for human consumption.

How often should I apply humic acid to my vegetable garden?

For vegetable gardens, a monthly application during the growing season is a practical starting point. Many gardeners apply humic acid at the beginning of the season during soil preparation, then follow up every 4–6 weeks through the growing cycle. The frequency depends on your soil's organic matter content — depleted soils may benefit from more frequent applications in the first season, while well-amended soils require less. Always follow product-specific application rate guidelines.

Can I use BioActiveGlo products on all types of plants?

Yes, BioActiveGlo humic and fulvic acid products are broadly compatible with vegetables, ornamentals, trees, shrubs, lawns, and container plants. The application rates and dilution ratios may vary by plant type — vegetables and high-yield crops may benefit from slightly higher rates, while ornamentals and established trees typically require less. Refer to individual product guidelines and start at the lower end of recommended rates when applying to a new plant type.

Is it safe to use these products around pets and children in the garden?

Humic and fulvic acids are naturally occurring organic compounds found in healthy soils. When used at recommended rates as directed for gardening applications, these products are generally safe for use in garden environments. Standard precautions apply: keep children and pets away from the garden during application and allow the area to dry before re-entry. Store products securely, out of reach of children. BioActiveGlo products are for gardening use only — not for human consumption.

What's the difference between fulvic acid powder and liquid?

Fulvic acid powder is concentrated and more economical for soil drenching large areas — you dissolve it in water before application. The liquid form is pre-dissolved, more convenient for foliar spraying, and ready to dilute further. Both forms contain the same active compound; the primary differences are in application convenience, storage life, and cost per application. Powders typically have a longer shelf life, while liquids allow for immediate use. Choose based on your application method and volume needs.

Product Usage

What are the recommended application rates for the powders?

Humic Acid Powder is typically applied at 2–4 lbs per 1,000 square feet for soil amendment, mixed into the top 2–4 inches of soil or dissolved in water for a soil drench. Fulvic Acid Powder is used at lower rates — 0.5–2 lbs per 1,000 sq ft — due to its higher potency per unit weight. Raw Ore applies at 5–10 lbs per 1,000 sq ft as a slow-release amendment. Always begin at the lower end of the recommended range and adjust based on observed soil conditions.

Can I mix fulvic acid with fertilizers?

In most cases, yes. Fulvic acid's chelating properties can actually improve the uptake efficiency of compatible fertilizers, particularly those containing trace minerals. However, avoid mixing with highly alkaline fertilizers, as the acidic pH of fulvic acid (3.0–4.0 in solution) can create unwanted chemical interactions. Always test a small batch first when combining products. We recommend a simple jar test: mix the two products in water, observe for 30 minutes, and proceed if there are no precipitates or visible reactions.

How should I store my BioActiveGlo products?

Store powders in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture. Once opened, reseal the bag tightly or transfer to an airtight container to prevent moisture absorption and clumping. Liquids should be stored at room temperature, away from extreme heat or freezing. Shelf life is typically 2–3 years for powders and 1–2 years for liquids when stored properly. Avoid storing in humid environments like garages in high-humidity climates.

How long does a 1lb bag last for a typical home garden?

For a standard 500–1,000 square foot home garden, a 1lb bag of Humic Acid Powder provides approximately 2–4 applications at recommended rates. A 1lb bag of Fulvic Acid Powder will last significantly longer due to its higher potency and lower application rates — potentially 6–12 applications for the same size garden. Many home gardeners find a 1lb bag sufficient for one full growing season of regular applications on a typical backyard vegetable plot.

Can I use these products in hydroponic systems?

Fulvic acid is commonly used in hydroponic and aquaponic systems due to its ability to chelate minerals and improve nutrient solution stability. Use only the fulvic acid products and at low rates (0.1–0.25 oz per gallon) to avoid disrupting EC (electrical conductivity) readings and pH balance. Humic acid is generally less suitable for hydroponic systems due to its larger molecular size and potential to clog drip emitters. Always monitor pH and EC closely when introducing any new amendment to a hydroponic setup.
