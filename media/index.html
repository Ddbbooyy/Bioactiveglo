About Danny Albert

Danny Albert is a precision engineer with more than 25 years of professional engineering experience across complex technical systems. After applying the disciplines of systems thinking, controlled methodology, and measurement-based development throughout his engineering career, Danny redirected that rigor toward an emerging area of agricultural science: the use of fulvic and humic acid biostimulants for soil restoration and plant vitality.

Over six-plus years of independent research — sourcing and testing ore compositions, developing formulation specifications, and conducting controlled field applications — Danny founded BioActiveGlo to bring engineering-grade methodology to the biostimulant category. His work is grounded in the premise that gardeners, farmers, and agricultural professionals deserve products that can be specified, verified, and understood rather than simply trusted on the basis of marketing claims.

Danny speaks and writes on soil biology, biostimulant science, precision agriculture, and the long-term economics of soil health investment. He is available for podcast interviews, panel discussions, event speaking, and media commentary. All media inquiries can be directed to media@bioactiveglo.com.

Speaking Topics

Soil Biology & Biostimulant Science

How humic and fulvic acids work at the molecular level — chelation, CEC, microbiome support — and what the research actually says about their use in agricultural settings.

Precision Agriculture & Soil Health

Applying engineering methodology to soil management: measurement, specification, controlled testing, and the data-driven approach to building productive soil systems.

Rebuilding Depleted Agricultural Soils

The scope of soil depletion in managed agricultural and garden settings, and a systematic framework for restoration using biological inputs and organic matter management.

The Future of Soil Amendments

Where the biostimulant category is headed, regulatory developments in the EU and US, and how precision formulation will differentiate quality products from commodity inputs.

Engineering Approaches to Research

The gap between peer-reviewed soil science and practical gardening application — how to read research critically and apply findings in real-world garden environments.
